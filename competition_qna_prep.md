# VisionMate Competition Q&A Prep

## 1. What problem does VisionMate solve?
It helps visually impaired users move more safely and independently by combining object detection, face recognition, voice navigation, audio feedback, and guardian tracking in one mobile app.

## 2. Why is this better than a white cane alone?
A white cane detects physical obstacles, but it cannot recognize people, guide navigation, or notify a guardian. VisionMate adds digital awareness and safety support.

## 3. Why is your solution different from other assistive apps?
Most existing tools solve one problem only. VisionMate combines multiple assistive features in one smartphone app, so the user does not need extra hardware.

## 4. How do you handle privacy?
Face recognition uses embeddings instead of storing raw face images. The design also prefers on-device AI for sensitive processing.

## 5. What works offline and what needs internet?
Object detection and face recognition are designed to run on-device. Login, maps, and guardian live-location sync may require internet.

## 6. Who is the target user?
Primary users are visually impaired smartphone users. Secondary users are guardians, family members, rehabilitation centers, and accessibility organizations.

## 7. How will you make money or sustain the product?
The model can be freemium for users, with premium guardian features and institutional licensing for NGOs, clinics, and support programs.

## 8. What is your go-to-market plan?
Start with local pilot users, then partner with disability NGOs, rehabilitation centers, and universities to test and refine the product.

## 9. What is the biggest risk?
Accuracy in low-light or unstable conditions, plus battery and performance limits on low-end phones.

## 10. How do you reduce that risk?
Use lightweight on-device models, keep the UI simple, rely on audio feedback, and test with real users in realistic environments.

## 11. What is your strongest technical advantage?
The app is built for real-world use on a regular Android phone, with on-device AI and hardware button shortcuts for blind-friendly interaction.

## 12. What should judges remember most?
VisionMate is an affordable, practical, and integrated mobile assistant that improves safety, independence, and guardian confidence.
