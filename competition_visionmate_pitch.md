# VisionMate Hackathon Pitch Deck

## Slide 1: Problem
- Global crisis: Over 2.2 billion people worldwide live with vision impairment.
- Cost barrier: Most smart assistive devices cost $500 to $3,000, making adoption difficult for the majority of users.
- Safety gap: Standard tools such as white canes do not provide real-time digital awareness, increasing accident risk and dependency.

## Slide 2: Solution
- VisionMate converts a standard smartphone into AI Eyes using on-device machine learning.
- Unique edge: one integrated app for object detection, offline face recognition, and guardian live sync.
- Hardware-free accessibility:
  - No extra wearables or sensors required
  - Physical button shortcuts for blind-friendly interaction
  - Volume Up double press = voice command / destination input
  - Volume Up triple press = save current location as favorite

## Slide 3: Opportunity
- Primary target: English-speaking visually impaired smartphone users (Phase 1).
- The gap: strong demand for an all-in-one, low-cost mobility assistant in urban markets.
- Scalability: Android-based architecture enables rapid rollout across international accessibility networks.

## Slide 4: Competition
- Rubric focus: clear competitive differentiation.

| Feature | Seeing AI / Lookout | Smart Canes | VisionMate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Live guardian sync | No | No | Yes (included) |
| Offline privacy | No (cloud dependent) | Yes | Yes (included) |
| Setup cost | $0 | $500+ | $0 |
| Non-touch UI via hardware shortcuts | Limited | No | Yes (included) |

## Slide 5: Business Model
- Institutional licensing: bulk access for NGOs, rehabilitation centers, and clinics.
- B2C premium: advanced safety analytics and real-time caregiver dashboards.
- Impact-driven revenue: high-volume, low-cost subscription model for sustainable scale.

## Slide 6: Go-To-Market
- Launch: partnerships with accessibility support groups and disability clinics.
- Distribution: direct APK and Play Store for English-literate urban populations.
- Retention: continuous model improvement from structured user feedback loops.

## Slide 7: Ask
- Funding goal: $10,000 for prototype scaling and global field testing.
- Milestone 1: launch a 100-user multi-city pilot and validate safety metrics.
- Milestone 2: reach 95% accuracy targets for real-time obstacle and face recognition in pilot conditions.

## Refined 3-Minute Script (Harvard HSIL Style)
### 0-30s: The Hook
Imagine being one of the 2.2 billion people globally who cannot move safely outside their homes. Today's technology offers help, but often at a price of up to $3,000, which many families cannot afford.

### 30-90s: The Solution
We built VisionMate. It transforms the smartphone already in your pocket into a life-saving AI assistant. Using on-device processing, VisionMate recognizes obstacles and familiar faces in real time, even without internet, while keeping user data private.

### 90-120s: The Differentiation
Unlike single-purpose tools, VisionMate combines object detection, offline face recognition, and guardian live sync in one accessible app. It supports non-touch interaction through hardware button shortcuts, so it stays practical for visually impaired users in real movement scenarios.

### 120-180s: The Close
We are seeking $10,000 to move from prototype to a 100-user multi-city pilot and validate real-world safety impact. Help us make independence a daily reality for visually impaired people, one smartphone at a time.

## Quick Presentation Tips
- Lead with the human problem, not the technology.
- Keep the demo sequence simple: object detection, face recognition, navigation, guardian sync.
- Do not spend too long on model names unless asked.
- Emphasize measurable impact, usability, and affordability.

## Data Credibility Reminder (For Q&A)
- Keep one backup slide with citation links for: global vision impairment statistics and assistive device pricing ranges.
- When quoting the 95% milestone, state clearly: this is a pilot target under defined test conditions.
